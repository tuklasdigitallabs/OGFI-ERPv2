# Phase 3 Deferred Go-Live Blockers For UAT

This document records Phase 3 release and go-live blockers that do not prevent the ERP from running locally or being demonstrated, but must be reviewed with finance, operations, and workforce users during actual UAT before Phase 3 can be represented as production release-ready.

## Deferred Blockers

| ID           | Area                                      | Deferred blocker                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            | Why deferred                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       | Required UAT decision/evidence                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ------------ | ----------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| P3-BLOCK-001 | Cash advances and petty cash              | Cash advance payment handoff must not reuse the supplier-only `PaymentRequest` model for employee/custodian advances. A generic payee registry and controlled non-supplier disbursement request foundation now exists for cash-advance and approved petty-cash request handoff, but production payment release, settlement, bank mutation, petty-cash ledger mutation from handoff, journal posting, and final UAT policy remain deferred. | F&B cash advances and petty-cash replenishment/disbursement flows are commonly issued to employees or branch custodians, while the current AP payment request schema is supplier-oriented. Treating employees as suppliers would weaken AP/payee controls and reporting, while the new handoff foundation preserves source linkage without releasing cash or posting accounting effects. | UAT must confirm payee taxonomy, allowed source workflows, SoD, approval route, evidence policy, payment-release separation, settlement/reversal behavior, and no journal/bank/petty-cash ledger mutation until an authorized release or fulfillment step. |
| P3-BLOCK-002 | Evidence uploads                          | Workflows that require evidence now share evidence-readiness metadata, a controlled source-record attachment-link table, permissioned link/list/archive service functions, metadata-link UI wiring across Phase 3, and a private binary upload/download foundation. Expense requests, AP invoices, supplier credit notes, payment requests, payment releases, branch cash deposits, bank reconciliations, cash advances, petty cash, workforce, and period close now support private evidence file upload, checksum capture, permissioned retrieval, and download audit through controlled evidence links. The configurable evidence-storage policy now defines the local-private provider, upload limit, MIME allowlist, scan-waiver mode, retention policy, recovery policy, and download-audit requirement. Production still needs storage-provider approval, scan or scan-waiver approval, recovery proof, and UAT evidence. | Phase 3 evidence workflows can be demonstrated with real private binary evidence while preserving metadata-only fallback for existing references. The policy is explicit and admin-visible, but production still needs owner signoff that local-private storage is acceptable or replaced, that the scan waiver or scanner is approved, that backup/restore covers the evidence root, and that UAT passes. | Confirm storage provider, retention matrix, upload limits, scan policy or waiver, access rules, recovery proof, and which workflows require binary evidence at each status; then execute evidence UAT scripts. |
| P3-BLOCK-003 | Budget source hooks                       | PR/PO/AP source lines now carry nullable budget-line allocation dimensions, PR line entry can capture active scoped budget lines, PO lines inherit PR budget allocation, AP invoice lines inherit scoped PO budget allocation, a non-mutating allocation resolver exists, PR approval, PO approval, and clean AP match transitions now create audited warning-mode budget commitment projections, and PR/PO/AP cancellation paths reverse existing source projections. Hard-block rollout, backfill, and UAT coverage remain incomplete. | The current budget service supports source-linked commitments, and expense requests carry budget-line IDs. PR/PO/AP records now have a stable allocation path and controlled source-transition projection/reversal hooks, but production rollout still needs migration/backfill policy, exception override testing, and finance/operations UAT before hard blocks can be enabled. | Confirm migration/backfill strategy, warning versus hard-block policy, exception override approval, source-transition test cases, and commitment creation/reversal UAT.                                                            |
| P3-BLOCK-004 | Supplier credits and AP settlement        | Supplier credit notes can be recorded and submitted for application review, but they do not yet reduce AP outstanding balances or settle supplier ledgers.                                                                                                                                                  | The AP workflow requires credit notes not to silently reduce invoices without traceable accounting entries. The current controlled foundation intentionally stops before supplier settlement and official journal posting.                                                                                                                                                         | Confirm credit-application workflow, approval route, reversal path, supplier statement reconciliation rules, journal/accounting treatment, and UAT cases for partial/full credit application.                                    |
| P3-BLOCK-005 | Payment release settlement                | Payment releases can be controlled and matched to reconciliation sources, but production settlement, AP balance reduction, and official accounting consequences remain gated.                                                                                                                               | Offline payment control is implemented, but bank API, AP settlement mutation, supplier-ledger settlement, and journal posting require finance-owner validation, binary evidence, reconciliation ownership, and UAT.                                                                                                                                                                | Confirm settlement policy, method-specific evidence, one/two-signatory rules, AP closeout behavior, reversal/recovery path, and payment-to-bank-to-ledger UAT.                                                                   |
| P3-BLOCK-006 | Bank/cash close exceptions                | Period close can identify and route close-readiness exceptions, payment releases can be matched to bank lines, and branch cash deposits can now be matched to inflow statement lines, but broader exception resolution and production close signoff still require UAT.                                          | Close readiness, lock/reopen routing, unmatched statement exception sync, and controlled reconciliation matching are implemented as foundations. Production close requires business users to prove reconciliation ownership, exception resolution, and fallback handling.                                                                                                             | Execute close UAT with unmatched, partially matched, waived, resolved, reopened, and relocked scenarios; collect finance/release signoff.                                                                                        |
| P3-BLOCK-007 | Workforce transfer and document retention | Workforce records, assignments, controlled primary-assignment handoffs, schedules, leave, overtime, and attendance exception approvals are implemented, but full employee transfer approval workflow and document upload/retention are not production-ready.                                                    | `III-008` confirms assignment maintenance as the foundation boundary and keeps full transfer approval routing pending. The controlled handoff foundation can end the current primary assignment the day before a new primary assignment starts, with reason/evidence metadata and audit, but it is not a full source/destination approval route. Binary workforce documents are also covered by `P3-BLOCK-002`. | Confirm employee transfer lifecycle, approval route, effective-date conflicts beyond primary handoff, rollback/reversal rules, document retention/access requirements, and UAT for source/destination branch managers.           |

Phase 3 must not be represented as production release-ready until these deferred blockers are either completed with evidence or formally waived by authorized owners with documented mitigation.
