## Scope Boundary

- No queueing functionality is included in Phase I or Phase 1.5.
- Do not introduce worker, scheduler, Redis, or queue-dependent release criteria unless a separate approved technical decision adds them.
- In-app notifications and manual project reminder scan behavior are the current release scope.
- Binary upload/download for all operational attachments remains deferred unless a specific deployed environment includes the approved shared attachment service.

## Rollback Decision Rules

Rollback or hold release when any of these occur:

- Authentication, scope switching, or direct URL authorization fails.
- Purchasing, receiving, transfer, wastage, stock adjustment, or inventory posting creates incorrect source-record status or ledger movement.
- Migration corrupts existing records or cannot be safely rerun.
- Backup cannot be restored in an isolated environment.
- Critical or blocker UAT defect remains open without approved mitigation.
- Required role/scope assignments cannot be verified for pilot users.

## Smoke Test Minimum

1. Sign in as requester, approver, and administrator.
2. Switch to the pilot branch and pilot warehouse contexts.
3. Create and submit a Purchase Request.
4. Approve a different user's Purchase Request.
5. Create or open a Purchase Order and verify status/source lineage.
6. Create and post a receiving record with accepted quantity.
7. Dispatch and receive a transfer.
8. Open stock balances and inventory ledger for the affected item/location.
9. Log, approve, post, and reverse one wastage or stock adjustment scenario where UAT scope includes it.
10. Open Operations Dashboard and drill down to source records.
11. Export one operational report and verify export audit.
12. Create/open a project task, blocker, milestone, and safe linked record.
13. Verify unauthorized user cannot see a restricted source record or restricted project.
