# Marketing Campaign, Promotion, and New-Item Workflow

## Campaign workflow

1. **Idea Intake** — capture request, objective, brand/branch scope, preliminary date, owner.
2. **Briefing** — define audience, channel, success measure, required branches, dependencies, and preliminary assets.
3. **Planning** — create tasks, dates, calendar entries, budget/reference links, and approvals.
4. **For Approval** — validate required decision makers and supporting information.
5. **In Production** — creative/content/rollout work proceeds through shared work items.
6. **Ready for Launch** — validate branch scope, assets, communications, dependencies, and required approvals.
7. **Scheduled / Live** — calendar-controlled activation and monitoring.
8. **Post-Campaign Review** — record results, issues, lessons, and closure status.

## Promotion controls

A promotion must define:

- Brand and eligible branch scope
- Start/end date and timezone/date-only behavior
- Mechanics and terms/conditions reference
- Required approval state
- Required POS/setup/communication readiness references where applicable
- Accountable owner and assignees

A promotion marked `Live` cannot automatically configure POS, inventory pricing, finance, or branch operations. It records readiness and links to authorized source tasks/records.

## New-item launch workflow

1. Idea / concept
2. Validation / R&D reference
3. Food cost / pricing reference
4. Operations and supplier readiness
5. Packaging / creative readiness
6. POS and branch setup reference
7. Training and communication
8. Soft launch
9. Full launch
10. Performance review

The Marketing module orchestrates visibility and assignments; underlying records remain source of truth in their respective modules.

## Cancellation and change

- Date, branch-scope, mechanic, or key campaign changes require an activity reason and may require re-approval depending on configured policy.
- Cancellation preserves work, assets, decisions, and performance context.
