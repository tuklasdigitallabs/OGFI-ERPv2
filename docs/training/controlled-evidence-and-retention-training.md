# Training Module — Controlled Evidence And Retention

**Audience:** Finance and workforce users, project-requirement owners, authorized reviewers, ERP Administrators, legal/compliance users, auditors, and support staff
**Duration:** 30 minutes for upload users; 45 minutes including administrator legal-hold practice
**Prerequisites:** A supported scoped source record; source-record upload authority; for the administrator exercise, `View evidence retention register`, active company scope, and separately assigned legal-hold permission with current privileged MFA assurance
**Related knowledge-base articles:** Uploading Supporting Documents Or Photo Evidence; Managing Evidence Retention And Placing A Legal Hold

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, participants can:

- Select an allowed evidence file and upload it to the correct source record.
- Interpret quarantine, scanning, available, rejected, failed, expired, unverified, and archived user-facing states.
- Explain why upload, download, archive, approval, and source-record actions have separate authority.
- Archive an optional evidence link without treating the action as file deletion.
- Review the confidential company register and distinguish view-only access from legal-hold placement.
- Place a preservation-only legal hold with current privileged MFA and explain why release and purge are unavailable.

## Demonstration flow

1. Open a supported source record in the correct company and scope.
2. Select `Upload Evidence`, choose an allowed file no larger than 25 MB, enter a caption, and submit it.
3. Point out `Upload received` and `Safety check in progress`; explain that the file cannot be downloaded while unavailable.
4. Refresh the record and identify an `Available`, rejected, failed, expired, or unverified result. Demonstrate download only with a clean, available file.
5. On an optional link, select `Archive`, enter the reason, and confirm that the link is archived while evidence is retained for audit.
6. Sign in with confidential-register view access and open `Admin` → `Evidence Retention`.
7. Review scope, metadata, source lineage, retention information, state badges, paging, and `Show legal holds only` without opening file bytes.
8. Compare a view-only record with one that offers `Place Legal Hold`.
9. Open `Place Legal Hold`, review the preservation warning, refresh MFA assurance if prompted, and enter the approved authority, case reference, and reason.
10. Select `Confirm Legal Hold` and confirm the `LEGAL HOLD` badge and success message.
11. Explain that the workspace cannot release a hold or physically purge evidence.

## Practice exercise

Upload a valid test document to an assigned training record, identify its current safety state, and state whether it may be downloaded. Then review two retention-register records: identify which is view-only, which can receive a hold, and why a purged record cannot be held. Authorized trainees may place a hold on the designated training evidence using the supplied mock instruction.

## Common errors and recovery

- Unsupported or oversized file: choose PDF, JPG/JPEG, PNG, WebP, or plain text at 25 MB or less.
- Upload window expired: start the upload again.
- Interrupted upload: check the connection and retry without sending the file through an unapproved channel.
- Storage at its limit: stop repeated attempts and contact an administrator.
- Safety check remains pending or processing repeatedly fails: give support the record, scope, time, and visible status.
- No upload or download action: verify live source-record permission and scope; a copied link or view-only access is insufficient.
- No hold action: verify the separate placement permission, active company scope, preservation state, and current privileged MFA assurance.
- Wrong hold or release request: stop and escalate. The current workspace has no hold-release or purge action.

## Completion check

- Participant uploads evidence to the correct record without claiming it changed approval, inventory, or financial state.
- Participant allows download only when the file is clean and `Available`.
- Participant explains that archive removes the source link but preserves stored evidence and audit history.
- Administrator identifies confidential company scope, view-only access, MFA requirements, and the no-release/no-purge boundary correctly.
