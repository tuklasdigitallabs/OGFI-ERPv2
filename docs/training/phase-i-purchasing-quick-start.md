# Training Module — Phase I Purchasing Quick Start

**Audience:** Purchasing officers, purchasing managers, and procurement support users  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Prerequisites:** Purchase Request, supplier quotation, Purchase Order, receiving visibility, report/export, and approval access as assigned  
**Related knowledge-base articles:** Creating a Purchase Request; Reviewing and approving a Purchase Request; Understanding Purchase Order statuses; Receiving a complete supplier delivery; Receiving a partial, short, damaged, or rejected delivery; How to export a report

## Learning objectives

By the end of this module, participants can:

- Review approved Purchase Requests for purchasing follow-up.
- Understand quotation and supplier recommendation boundaries.
- Track Purchase Order status from draft through issue and receiving.
- Explain receiving variance handling and PO outstanding quantity.
- Export scoped purchasing and receiving records.

## Demonstration flow

1. Open `Purchase Requests` and filter approved requests.
2. Review the source request, line item, requester, location, and audit trail.
3. Open supplier quote or recommendation pages where permitted.
4. Open `Purchase Orders` and explain `DRAFT`, `PENDING_APPROVAL`, `APPROVED`, `ISSUED`, `PARTIALLY_RECEIVED`, `FULLY_RECEIVED`, `CANCELLED`, and `CLOSED`.
5. Record supplier issue/send evidence where applicable.
6. Open `Receiving` to review accepted, rejected, damaged, short, and outstanding quantities.
7. Export a scoped purchasing or receiving CSV.

## Practice exercise

Follow an approved Purchase Request through quote review, PO status review, supplier issue, and receiving variance review. Explain which record is the source of truth at each step.

## Common errors and recovery

- Treating PR approval as supplier commitment: create or review the PO before supplier commitment.
- Treating PO approval as delivery: receiving remains a separate stock-in workflow.
- Missing issued status before receiving: record supplier issue/send when required.
- Exporting the wrong scope: check filters and active location before export.

## Completion check

- Participant can explain PR to PO lineage, PO status meaning, receiving variance impact, and why accepted receiving quantities drive inventory movement.
